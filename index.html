~/about

Securing systems, not just running them

I'm a final-semester Networking & Security student at the Arab Open University in Dammam — but I didn't start in IT. I spent years in industrial quality inspection, running NDT and dimensional inspections on oil-field projects, which taught me process discipline and attention to detail long before I ever touched a switch. That mindset is exactly what security work demands.

My cooperative training in Worley's IT department put me inside real enterprise infrastructure: imaging and deploying Windows across company devices, managing accounts and access rights in Active Directory, resolving tickets in ServiceNow, and working hands-on with LAN infrastructure, switch and server rooms, and VoIP. I'm now looking for a full-time IT role where I can contribute from day one.

I gravitate to the technical side of defense: traffic analysis in Wireshark, reconnaissance and scanning with Nmap, and the firewall and IDS configuration work that security teams live in every day. Alongside security, I'm genuinely interested in AI — both as a daily working tool and for how machine-learning systems are changing what defenders have to protect.
